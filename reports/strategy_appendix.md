# Strategy Appendix

## Executive Summary

- This appendix consolidates the matrix-style outputs that bankers typically want to scan quickly: bank comparison, India sector priorities, borrower archetypes, and model-confidence registers.
- It is designed as a portfolio-grade supplement to the roadmap, product playbook and bank note rather than a standalone memo.

## Assumption Register

| Area | Assumption | Treatment | Confidence | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Country financing anchor | USD 300bn annual transition financing need used as the central illustrative anchor. | Illustrative scenario input | Medium | Sets the order-of-magnitude envelope for subsector allocation and capital-pool sizing. |
| Subsector splits | Allocation weights translate the country anchor across eight transition subsectors. | Illustrative scenario input | Medium | Determines where products are shown to scale first. |
| Capital-channel mix | Each subsector is split across bank balance sheet, public markets, blended / DFI, and carbon-linked pools. | Illustrative scenario input | Medium-Low | Frames where each bank can realistically originate or distribute capital. |
| Borrower archetypes | Corporate profiles are fictional but realistic and are not intended to represent actual clients. | Design choice | High | Allows product-stack demonstration without client confidentiality concerns. |
| Product scoring | Rule-based scoring prioritises explainability over statistical optimisation. | Method choice | High | Keeps the work sample interpretable for strategy and origination discussions. |
