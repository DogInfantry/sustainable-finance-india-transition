## What This Repository Is

This repository is a structured research and analysis project examining **how India finances its transition to a low-carbon economy** — covering green bonds, ESG frameworks, climate risk in Indian capital markets, transition finance instruments, and the regulatory architecture being built by SEBI and RBI.

India needs **~$10 trillion in cumulative climate investment by 2070** to meet its net-zero commitment. Currently, tracked finance flows cover only ~25% of what's required. This repo explores the gap — what's missing, why it's missing, and what institutional and market mechanisms can close it.

> Built as part of an MBA-level research and portfolio project. All analysis, frameworks, and models are original work.

## Core Research Themes

### 1. Green Finance Architecture in India
India's green finance ecosystem is nascent but accelerating. This section covers:
- **SEBI's BRSR framework** — Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting, mandated for top-1000 listed companies
- **RBI's green deposit guidelines** — draft rules shaping how banks deploy green capital
- **India's sovereign green bond programme** — ₹16,000 crore issuance in FY2023, what it means for the market
- **The taxonomy gap** — why India still lacks a formal green taxonomy and how this constrains capital flows

### 2. Transition Finance — The Hard-to-Abate Problem
Green finance funds what's already clean. Transition finance funds the *shift*. India's hard-to-abate sectors — **steel, cement, coal power, chemicals** — need a different instrument stack:
- Transition bonds and sustainability-linked bonds (SLBs)
- Blended finance structures for derisking
- PFC and REC as state-backed transition finance conduits
- The $3.5 trillion global transition finance market vs. India's current mobilisation

### 3. Climate Risk in Indian Capital Markets
- Physical risk exposure in Nifty 50 constituents (floods, heat stress, water scarcity)
- Transition risk — stranded asset calculus for coal and fossil fuel-linked equities
- Portfolio-level stress testing under 1.5°C and 2°C scenarios
- Alignment with TCFD disclosure expectations

### 4. The Just Transition Dimension
The energy transition is not just environmental — it's socioeconomic. India has ~3 million workers in coal and fossil fuel sectors, concentrated in states like Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, and Odisha. This analysis covers:
- Workforce displacement estimates by sector and region
- Financial instruments with social tagging (green bonds + just criteria)
- SEBI and RBI's role in embedding just transition into regulatory frameworks

## Original Frameworks in This Repo

**Transition Readiness Matrix** — A sector-level assessment tool scoring India's major industries on four axes: decarbonisation pathway clarity, financing availability, regulatory enablement, and workforce transition readiness.

**Blended Finance Architecture Map** — Visual framework mapping the capital stack for Indian climate projects: MDB senior debt → DFI mezzanine → private equity → retail green bonds.

**ESG Materiality Heat Map (India-specific)** — Which ESG factors are financially material for which Indian sectors, calibrated against BRSR disclosure categories.

## How to Use This Repository

**If you're a researcher or analyst:** Start with `/frameworks` for the conceptual scaffolding, then `/analysis` for sector-specific findings. Data is in `/data` in cleaned `.csv` format with source documentation.

**If you're a policymaker or ESG practitioner:** The `/policy` folder has annotated summaries of SEBI, RBI, and NITI Aayog frameworks with gap analysis and international comparisons.

**If you're an investor or portfolio manager:** The `/models` folder has scenario-based financial models for climate risk integration into Indian equity and debt portfolios.
